Neighborhoods of Haifa

Downtown


Zvi Roger / CC BY

The downtown area contains many historical sites including the old city which was surrounded by a wall and built by Dahar El Omar in the 18th century.

Namal Haifa (Haifa Port) is the largest of the three major seaports in the country. It has been in use for centuries, and still to this day cruises and imports from all over the world dock here.
Paris Square - one of the main nightlife areas in Haifa, with street music and lively pubs and restaurants, anyone is in for a good time
Carmelit - currently Israel's only underground railway, built in 1959 and continuously used as the main transportation to go up and down the mountain in less than 15 minutes

Carmel Center


deror_avi / CC BY-SA

Stella Maris - Carmelite monastery featuring an elevated altar, muraled dome roof and ornate stained-glass windows
Visit the Bahai Gardens, with 19 terraces of geometric flower beds, climbing to the domed Bahai shrine on Mt Carmel.
Haifa Zoo
Come take a walk on the Louis Promenade for incredible breath-taking views of Haifa and the bay. On a clear day you can see all the way to Akko, Nahariya, and even Lebanon!
Haifa Auditorum
Museums - Mane Katz and Tikotin - the only museum in the Middle East dedicated only to Japanese art
Sculptures Garden

German Colony


Noal242 / CC BY-SA

The German Colony was established in Ottoman Haifa in 1868 by the German Templers. It was the first of several colonies established by the group in the Holy Land. Others were founded in Sarona near Jaffa, Galilee and Jerusalem.

Templer buildings - travel back in time to these incredible 19th century German buildings, learn about the culture that helped build the city
Haifa City Museum - Haifa's cultural past, housed in an authentic Templer-era building

Hadar


Tomer chelouche / Public domain

Shuk Talpiot - Haifa's open air market, where you can find delicious fresh produce, fragrant spices, vintage treasures, and more
Haifa Theater
Take the time to visit one of Haifa's many museums - Madatech - child-friendly science museum, the Children's Museum, Haifa Museum of Art - Israeli and international contemporary art, Holocaust Museum, Hermann Struck Museum
Bauhaus
Beit HaGefen - the Arab-Jewish Cultural Center is a multi-cultural organisation that aims to bring together Arabs and Jews and promote coexistence and tolerance
HaZikaron Park

Bat Galim


Zvi Roger / CC BY

Bat Galim was the first point of Jewish settlement in modern Haifa. It is also known for its promenade and sandy beaches.

Take a break at Shikmona Beach, an archeological site and marine biology coast, with man-made pools perfect for taking a relaxing dip in the water.
Cable cars
Elijah's Cave - the grotto believed by some to be the location where the prophet Elijah once took shelter
National Maritime Museum - founded in 1953, this museum features exhibits on maritime history, ocean archeology and pirates.

Hof HaCarmel


Guy Luria / CC BY-SA

Some of the best beaches in the city are located on the west side of the mountain, and it's a must to vist at least one. Dado and Student Beaches are local favorites, but feel free to explore the 21KM of magnificent coastlines surrounding the city.
Hecht Park - come take a break from the hustle and bustle of the city and take in the scenic views from this new little park
Sami Ofer Stadium
