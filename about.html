About


Planning

Haifa has a Mediterranean climate with hot, humid summers and cool, rainy winters. Between May and October it's best to dress lightly, the summer heat is no joke! From December to February/March, the area cools down significantly and downpours are to be expected, so break out the raincoat and boots!

Itineraries

1 Day in Haifa

If you only have one day to spend in Haifa, check out the Bahai Gardens for a free walking tour throughout the gardens. When you finish with the tour, head over the Shuk Talpiot for some great live music and food stalls. In the evening, downtown is a great place to just chill and have a beer with some friends.


2 Days in Haifa

Day 1
Head down to one of Haifa's many beaches for some relaxing and some great waves. The crystal blue waters are something seriously to see. Bat Galim beach or any beach in Hof Hacarmel are considered to be the best for swimming and sunbathing.
For those visiting during the winter, or just want an indoor activity, I highly suggest visiting the Madatech Museum of Science, Technology, and Space. Not just for kids to enjoy, with tons of interactive exhibits and an outdoor science park everyone can have fun while learning something new.


3 Days in Haifa

Days 1+2
For those more adventurous, there are plenty of great hiking spots throughout the city that lead to some of the most breathtaking sights. I would recommend Nahal Siach, Nahal Ben Dor, or Nahal Kati'a. Givat Eizim is a wonderful place for an easy walk in nature with a great spot to watch the sunset.
